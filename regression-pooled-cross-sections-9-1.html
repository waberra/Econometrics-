What Is a Pooled Cross Section?

Combine independent cross sections from different time periods
In a pooled cross section, we observe different individuals in each period, but we stack all periods together and treat them as one enlarged sample with a time indicator.
Use: Policy changes, before/after comparisons, tracking average outcomes over time.
Example: Housing prices in 1978 and 1981, with different households in each year.

Brief Practical Discussion

Pooled CS vs Panel Data
Pooled cross sections follow different individuals over time; panel data follow the same individuals. Pooled CS is simpler, but you cannot track individual dynamics‚Äîonly changes in the distribution and conditional means over time.
Use: When repeated independent surveys are available for several years.
Rule of thumb: Time dummies in pooled cross sections are great for simple before‚Äìafter comparisons with controls.
