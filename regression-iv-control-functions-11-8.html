A control function is a constructed regressor (often a residual or index) that captures the part of the endogenous variable correlated with u. Conditional on this control, x becomes exogenous.
Use: Endogenous regressors, sample selection, endogenous treatment, non-linear first stages.
Example: Wage equation with schooling endogenous; include residual from schooling equation as an extra regressor.

Assumptions & Limitations

The control function approach relies on a correctly specified first-stage model and valid excluded variables. It is not magic: bad instruments or mis-specified Probit/Logit stages give bad control functions.
Use: When IV is natural but nonlinear models (e.g. Probit/Logit) are convenient.
Tip: Always report and discuss the excluded variables and first-stage fit.
