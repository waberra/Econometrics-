With panel data, unobserved time-invariant effects (ci) can be differenced out or conditioned on. Time variation in policies or prices can provide quasi-experimental (instrumental) variation.
Use: Repeated observations for firms, individuals, schools, regions.
Example: Wage panels, firm productivity panels, regional policy adoption over time.

Panel data help only if there is meaningful time variation that can be treated as exogenous. Instrument sets must vary across time and/or units and satisfy relevance and exogeneity.
Use: Policy reforms, staggered adoption, time-varying cost shifters.
Tip: If an instrument does not change over time, it cannot instrument within/differenced variation.
