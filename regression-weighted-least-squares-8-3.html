Group Averages & Different Group Sizes

When each observation is a group mean based on ni units, larger groups have smaller error variance. WLS gives larger weight to larger groups.
Use: School-level averages, firm-level averages, regional aggregates.
Example: A district with 400 students gets 4 times the weight of a district with 100 students.

Brief Practical Discussion

WLS vs OLS + Robust SEs
WLS is most useful when you have a strong model for how Var(u|Z) changes. If the form is uncertain, OLS with robust standard errors is safer.
Use: Clear theoretical or sampling-based reasons for the weights (e.g. group sizes).
Rule of thumb: Use WLS when the variance function is known up to scale; otherwise, stick with OLS and robust SEs.
